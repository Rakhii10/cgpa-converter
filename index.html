Frequently Asked Questions
VTU (Visvesvaraya Technological University) uses: Percentage = (CGPA − 0.75) × 10. For example, a CGPA of 8.5 gives (8.5 − 0.75) × 10 = 77.5%. This is officially prescribed by VTU for all affiliated colleges in Karnataka.
AICTE recommends: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. So 8.0 CGPA = 76%. This is the most widely accepted formula in India for job applications and government forms when your university has no specific formula.
Always use your university's official formula. If HR asks for a general conversion and your university has none, use CGPA × 9.5 — accepted by most Indian companies including TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and government PSUs.
Anna University uses: Percentage = CGPA × 10. A CGPA of 8.2 = 82%. This is the simplest and most straightforward conversion, officially prescribed for all affiliated engineering colleges in Tamil Nadu.
Most companies have a CGPA cutoff of 6.5–7.0. MNCs like Google and Microsoft look for 7.5+. PSUs like ISRO, BARC require 6.5+. A CGPA of 7.5+ makes you eligible for 95% of campus placement opportunities.
Autonomous colleges may follow their parent university's formula or define their own. Most use CGPA × 10 or CGPA × 9.5. Always check your marksheet or academic office for your institution's official formula.
